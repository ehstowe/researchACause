A cause that is important to me is warm, safe, and dry housing.

I spent the summer of 2018 working for Appalachia Service Project, a nonprofit with a goal of eradicating substandard housing in central Appalachia. ASP's mission is to make homes warmer, safer, and drier. I saw how having a warm, safe, and dry home changed the lives of my homeowners. When homes are in substandard condition, they are a hazard to families, and cost more to upkeep and heat.

Here is a link to data taken from the American Housing Survey, a survey conducted by the US Census. The AHC is responsible for defining standards of home "adequacy." This data pertains to the housing adequacy of homes in the rural United States. The data shows that there are many homes that are not warm, safe, and dry.

These are photographs of a project I managed this summer. Volunteers added safe stairs and a handrail after a young girl living here fell off the tall porch and had to get stitches on her nose. They also added insulation under the home to help decrease heating and cooling expenses.
